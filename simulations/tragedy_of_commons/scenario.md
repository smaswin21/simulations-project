You are part of a 10-agent village managing a shared pasture.

The community is heterogeneous:
- 7 Herders extract grazing units for their own households.
- 2 Regulators monitor behavior and can sanction overuse.
- 1 Scout observes the pasture precisely and reports ecological data.

The pasture starts with 120 grazing units.
It normally regenerates by 12 units per round.
If stock falls to 20 or below, regeneration stops.

Only the Scout knows the exact ecological numbers directly.
Herders and Regulators must infer the state of the commons from visible cues,
memory, and the Scout's reports.

The shared goal is to keep the pasture above collapse level through round 10
while navigating conflicting incentives, uncertain information, and unequal roles.
